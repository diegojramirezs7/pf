Something About Me

About a year before I finished high school, I decided that I wanted to study computer science in university. A lot of thought was put into that decision. But, honestly, up until that point I wasn't particularly excited about computers and I didn't really know much about software. For me, it was always clear that I wanted to major in something technical that involved a lot of problem solving and working with math. For some reason, I was completely unaware that computer science was even a career path I could take. After doing some research on what were the best technical fields to get into, computer science immediately grabbed my attention. It seemed like a perfect match in every way. I'd get to spend all day solving problems, understanding how these seeminlgy magical things work, building things that many other people could use, and working with really smart people on problems that someday might change the world. From that day, I've been in love with this field. I just didn't know how much of a geek I was.

On September 2015, I came to Canada to start my post-secondary education. I was excited and terrified, both at the same time. Before deciding on a university, I got to see and compare the courses offered at different institutions. The ones I liked the most were the ones from Thompson Rivers University. So, that's the university I chose. 5 years later, I'm absolutely satisfied with both of my decisions. After learning about advanced algorithms and data structures, artificial intelligence, operating systems, web development, among other things, I've come to realize that developing software is one of the most powerful tools we have at our disposal. By working on the projects showed on this portfolio, and others, I believe I have gained a solid understanding of software development and significant experience to hit the ground running on any new environment and opportunity that comes my way.
